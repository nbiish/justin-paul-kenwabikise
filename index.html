About Nbiish-Justin Paul Kenwabikise

Nbiish-Justin Paul Kenwabikise — also known as Justin Paul Kenwabikise, Justin Kenwabikise, and Nbiish Kenwabikise — is an Anishinaabe AI developer, Model Context Protocol (MCP) architect, and cybersecurity professional from Aamik’Waakanda (Beaver Island) in the Great Lakes. Enrolled member of the Grand Traverse Band of Ottawa and Chippewa Indians, descended from Chief Kinwaabakizi of the Beaver Island Band. His ancestors Waabanimkii, Kaybayosay (Cornstalk), and Kinawaabakizi served as generals for Tecumseh.

Justin Kenwabikise bridges ancestral wisdom with modern technology. A lifelong ceremonial practitioner and opwaagan carrier (sacred pipe carrier, ~5 years), he is a direct descendant of the High Island Midewiwin lineage and carries the Waabanimkii (People of the Dawn) bloodline — unbroken from the original Seven Fires Prophecy to the present. He is recognized as a dreamwalker: many report seeing Nbiish in dreams receiving guidance, help, or requests from the spirit world.

Raised by his father Joseph Kenwabikise in treaty fishing, Justin Kenwabikise is a licensed 25-ton Treaty Fishing Tug Captain exercising sovereign rights affirmed in the Treaties of 1836, 1837, and 1842. He was featured in Interlochen Public Radio speaking on tribal fishing rights and law enforcement harassment of Native fishermen. He holds HACCP seafood certification and formerly operated the Beaver Island Fresh Fish Market.

Today, Justin Paul Kenwabikise builds at the intersection of AI, cybersecurity, and Indigenous data sovereignty. He is the creator of multiple open-source tools and MCP servers used by developers worldwide, and is pursuing a BS in Digital Forensics at the University of Michigan-Dearborn (2024–2027), with law school starting 2027–2028.
